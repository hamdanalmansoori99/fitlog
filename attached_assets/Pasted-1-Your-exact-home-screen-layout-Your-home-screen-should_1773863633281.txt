1. Your exact home screen layout

Your home screen should feel like a mission control center, not a dashboard dump.

Top section

Put this at the top:
	•	Greeting: Good evening, Hamdan
	•	Small status line: You’re 78% on track this week
	•	Primary CTA button: Start Workout
	•	Secondary CTA: Log Meal

This should instantly answer:
	•	how am I doing?
	•	what should I do now?

Card 1: Today’s Mission

This should be the main card.

Example:
	•	Today’s Mission
	•	Push Day + hit 180g protein
	•	Estimated time: 52 min
	•	You missed 1 session this week, so this workout matters

Buttons:
	•	Start Workout
	•	Ask Coach

This card should be dynamic based on:
	•	current split
	•	missed sessions
	•	weekly goal
	•	macro target

Card 2: Weekly Progress

Show 3 simple progress bars:
	•	Workouts: 4/5 completed
	•	Protein days hit: 3/7
	•	Calories on target: 4/7

Then under it:
	•	1 workout left to hit your weekly target

This creates urgency.

Card 3: Streak / Momentum

Simple but strong.
	•	5 day streak 🔥
	•	Best streak: 9 days
	•	Don’t break it today

Keep this visible.

Card 4: Smart Coach Insight

One short, strong insight only.

Examples:
	•	Your chest volume is down 14% from last week
	•	You’re under protein by 32g today
	•	Your last leg workout was 6 days ago
	•	Recovery looks low — reduce intensity today

Button:
	•	See Recommendation

Do not make this a generic chat preview.

Card 5: Recent Wins

This is for dopamine.

Examples:
	•	New PR: Incline Press +2.5kg
	•	3 workouts completed this week
	•	Calories on target 4 days in a row

This should rotate.

Bottom section

Quick actions:
	•	Start workout
	•	Log meal
	•	View progress
	•	Ask coach

That’s it. Keep it tight.

2. How your AI should actually work

This is the biggest thing. Your AI should not feel like “ChatGPT inside a gym app.”

It should feel like a decision engine using real user data.

Inputs the AI should use

Your coach logic should read:
	•	goal: fat loss / muscle gain / maintenance
	•	training split
	•	completed workouts this week
	•	missed workouts
	•	current day in plan
	•	exercise history
	•	volume trends
	•	PR history
	•	calories today
	•	protein today
	•	bodyweight trend
	•	sleep/recovery if you track it
	•	soreness/readiness if you ask user

What the AI should produce

Not long essays. Just practical outputs:

A. Daily recommendation

Example:
	•	You missed Pull Day yesterday. Do it today to stay on track.
	•	You’re under protein and calories. Keep training moderate and eat before bed.

B. Workout adjustment

Example:
	•	Bench volume is trending up well. Increase top set by 2.5kg next session.
	•	Fatigue looks high. Keep same weight and reduce 1 accessory set.

C. Nutrition correction

Example:
	•	You still need 41g protein. Best options: chicken wrap, Greek yogurt, whey shake.

D. Plateau detection

Example:
	•	Your bodyweight has stayed flat for 14 days while calories are below target only 3/7 days. Consistency is the issue, not calories.

E. Missed-session recovery

Example:
	•	You’re 2 sessions behind. Combine Push + light accessories tomorrow instead of skipping again.

Best AI quick actions

Instead of blank chat, add buttons like:
	•	What should I do today?
	•	Adjust my workout
	•	Check my nutrition
	•	Why am I not progressing?
	•	Make tomorrow’s plan
	•	I feel tired today

That will massively improve usage.

Tone of the AI

Should sound like:
	•	concise
	•	confident
	•	data-aware
	•	coach-like

Not:
	•	robotic
	•	overly motivational
	•	generic

Good example

You’re behind on your weekly workout goal and protein is low today. Do your 45-minute Push session and aim for one more 35g protein meal tonight.

Bad example

Consistency is key. Keep pushing toward your goals and remember to eat healthy foods.

3. Your killer feature

You need one thing people remember.

The best killer feature for FitLog is:

Auto-Coach Mode

A system that tells the user exactly what to do next based on their real logs.

That means every day the app can say:
	•	what workout to do
	•	whether to increase weight
	•	whether they’re behind on calories/protein
	•	whether they should deload
	•	whether recovery is low
	•	how to adjust if they missed sessions

This is much stronger than “AI chat.”

Your positioning becomes:

FitLog is the workout tracker that tells you exactly what to do next.

That is way better than:

workout logger + meals + AI

Why this is the right killer feature

Because most apps either:
	•	track
	•	or coach
	•	or do meals

Very few make the tracking directly create actionable coaching.

That’s your edge.

	•	know what to do next

5. How to make the workout flow elite

This needs to feel faster than Strong or Notes.

Add these:
	•	auto-fill last reps/weight
	•	one-tap set complete
	•	swipe to complete set
	•	persistent active workout bar
	•	quick rest buttons: +30s, Skip, Restart
	•	next exercise preview
	•	easy reorder/remove exercise
	•	finish workout summary with PRs and volume

Ideal flow

User opens app:
	•	sees Start Push Day
	•	taps
	•	first exercise already loaded
	•	last performance shown
	•	logs with 1 tap
	•	rest timer starts automatically
	•	next exercise is obvious

You want almost zero friction.
